**Severity**: LOW — odd but public parameter value; emits first+last row
instead of zero rows.
**Status**: Verified
**Branch**: master (direct, per the recorded codex-loop precedent)
**Commit**: (filled in at commit)

## Evidence
`src/PwshTokenCompressor.psm1` (Format-PtcTable as of 86f990e): the index
slice `$Rows[0..($take - 1)]` with `$take = 0` evaluates `0..-1`, which
PowerShell resolves as first-and-last. Codex probe: `1..5 |
Compress-PtcObject -MaxItems 0` emitted rows 1 and 5 plus `+5 more`; a
single row was emitted twice. Introduced by the 86f990e index-slice
conversion (Select-Object -First 0 previously yielded zero rows).

## Approach
`$sliced = ($take -gt 0) ? @($Rows[0..($take - 1)]) : @()`, used by both
the property-less and table branches.

  `\r`; fixed with `\r?` and re-proven: FAILS with the unguarded slice,
  PASSES with the fix.
